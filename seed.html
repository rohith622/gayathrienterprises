Seeds play a vital role in agriculture as they are the foundation for all crop production. A seed contains a living embryo that grows into a healthy plant when it receives the right amount of moisture, sunlight, and nutrients from the soil. The success of any farming activity largely depends on the quality of seeds used. High-quality seeds ensure good germination, strong plant growth, and higher yields. They also help in producing crops that are resistant to diseases, pests, and adverse weather conditions. By using scientifically developed seeds, farmers can increase their productivity and profitability while ensuring food security for the growing population

There are different types of seeds used in agriculture, such as hybrid, certified, foundation, breeder, and organic seeds. Each type serves a specific purpose in maintaining crop quality and yield. Hybrid seeds are produced by crossing two different varieties to achieve better productivity and resistance to diseases. Certified seeds are tested and approved by agricultural authorities to meet quality standards. Using such seeds not only improves the quantity of the harvest but also enhances the quality of the produce.
