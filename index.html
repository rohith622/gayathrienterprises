Pesticides are chemical or biological substances used to control pests.

They protect crops from insects, weeds, fungi, and other harmful organisms.

Pesticides help farmers increase crop yield and quality.

They are classified into different types based on their use.

Insecticides are used to kill or control harmful insects.

Herbicides are used to control unwanted weeds in the field.

Fungicides protect plants from fungal diseases like rust and mildew.

Rodenticides are used to control rats and mice in farmlands.

Pesticides can be synthetic chemicals or natural bio-pesticides.

Bio-pesticides are eco-friendly and safe for the environment.

They are made from natural materials such as plants, bacteria, and minerals.

Proper use of pesticides ensures healthy crop growth.

Overuse of pesticides can harm soil and water quality.

Farmers should always follow recommended dosages.

Protective measures like gloves and masks should be used while spraying.

Pesticides should be stored safely away from food and water.

Government regulations monitor the safe use of pesticides.

Pesticides play a key role in food security and agriculture.

They help reduce crop loss due to pests and diseases.

Integrated pest management combines pesticides with natural control methods.

This method reduces pesticide use and protects the environment.

Farmers should rotate pesticide use to avoid resistance in pests.

Training and awareness programs help farmers use pesticides safely.

Seeds are the basic input for agriculture and crop production.

They are the carriers of genetic information of plants.

Good quality seeds ensure healthy crop growth and better yield.

Seeds can be classified as hybrid seeds, open-pollinated seeds, and genetically modified seeds.

Hybrid seeds are produced by cross-breeding two different parent plants.

They provide higher yields and better resistance to diseases.

Open-pollinated seeds are naturally pollinated and can be saved for replanting.

Genetically modified seeds are engineered for specific traits like pest resistance.

Certified seeds are tested for purity, germination, and quality.

Seed treatment with fungicides or pesticides helps protect against diseases.

Proper storage of seeds is important to maintain their viability.

Seeds should be kept in a cool, dry, and pest-free environment.

Farmers should select seeds suitable for their soil and climate conditions.

High-yielding seeds play a major role in increasing farm productivity.

Vegetable seeds include tomato, brinjal, chili, and okra varieties.

Cereal seeds include paddy, wheat, maize, and millet varieties.

Pulses seeds include red gram, green gram, black gram, and chickpea.

Oilseed varieties include groundnut, sunflower, mustard, and soybean.

Seed replacement at regular intervals is necessary for higher yield.

Government agencies promote certified seed distribution programs.

Quality seeds reduce the need for excess fertilizers and pesticides.

Seed technology has helped in developing drought and pest-resistant varieties.

Farmers should avoid using old or uncertified seeds.

Fertilizers are substances added to soil to provide essential nutrients for plant growth.

They help in increasing the yield and quality of crops.

Fertilizers supply nutrients like Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium which are vital for plants.

They are mainly of two types: organic fertilizers and chemical fertilizers.

Organic fertilizers include compost, manure, and bio-fertilizers.

Chemical fertilizers are produced using industrial processes.

Some common chemical fertilizers are Urea, DAP, and Potash.

Urea is the most widely used nitrogen fertilizer in India.

Phosphorus-based fertilizers like DAP help in root development.

Potash fertilizers are important for fruit and flower formation.

Balanced fertilizer use ensures better productivity of soil.

Excessive use of chemical fertilizers can harm soil health.

Farmers are encouraged to use fertilizers in the right quantity.

Integrated nutrient management combines organic and chemical fertilizers.

This practice helps in maintaining soil fertility for a long time.

Fertilizers play a vital role in modern agriculture.

They help in meeting the food demand of the growing population.

India is one of the largest consumers of fertilizers in the world.

Government provides subsidies to farmers for fertilizers.

Proper storage of fertilizers is important to avoid spoilage.

Moisture should be avoided while storing fertilizers.

Farmers should always check the quality before buying fertilizers.

Bio-fertilizers are becoming popular as eco-friendly alternatives.

They improve soil structure and microbial activity.
